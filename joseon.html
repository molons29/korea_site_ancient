Joseon

The Joseon dysnasty was founded in 1392 by Yi Seonggye(이승계), and it lasted for nearly 500 years, until it was replaced by the Korean empire in 1897.

Founding

In the late 14th century, while the power of the preceding kingdom, Goryeo, was waning from years of attacks, the royal court of the kingdom split into 2 conflicting factions, one led by general Yi and the otehr by general Choe. Yi, while leading an attack on the liaodong peninsula, turned back and initiated a coup d'état in Gaegyeong, overthrowing king Woo. He later killed both king Woo and his heir, taking his place on the throne.

Power struggles

Throughout the middle period of the Joseon dynasty, Korea faced many hardships, both internal and external. Internally, a faction called the Sarim faction, gained control of the government while king Seonjo(선조) was in power. The Sarim faction then split into 2 opposing factions called the Easterners and the Westerners, the former of which then split into 2 more factions called the southerners and northerners. In the 1600s the Westerners permenantly split into the Noron and Soron factions. The alteration of power between these factions, led to charges of treason, purges and a vicious cycle of revenge.

In 1590, Toyotomi Hideyashi, a prominent Japanese General and politician, invaded Korea, intending to use it as a stepping stone to try and invade China. By using european firearms, and due to the internal squabbles of the Korean factions, Joseon's troops were overpowered and the Japanese occupied the south of the peninsula including seoul and pyongyang. The invasion was eventually slowed by Yi Sun-sin, an admiral, destroying the invasion fleet, as well as guerrilla combat to fight the ground troops.

Late Joseon

After invasion from the Japanese, Joseon experienced nearly 200 years of peace, and the emergence of Silhak(실크), or practical learning, which advocated a reform of the taxation, science and agriculture, in the aim of rebuilding society after it had been devastated by attacks. Kim Yuk(김유욱), king Hyeonjong(현종)'s chief minister implemented the reforms proposed by the Silhak, which proved extremely beneficial to both the state and the commonfolk.

Later, the 2 kings, Yeongjo(영조) and Jeongjo(정조), would bring about a second renaissance of the Joseong dynasty, establishing a royal library called Gyujanggak(규장각) which improved the country's cultural and political standing, and also establishing bold social initiatives, like opening government postitions to people of a lower class. All these developements were fully supported by the Silhak, who during this time encouraged the people to reflect on state tradition and lifestyle and initiated studies of Korea's history, geography and language.
